Trinity Numerica 2025

Celebrating Mathematical Excellence

Invitation Letter

Dear Students,

It gives me great pleasure to invite you to the Trinity Intra-School Math Competition. At Trinity, we believe mathematics is not only about numbers but also about building problem-solving skills, logical reasoning, and a spirit of healthy competition.

This event is designed to challenge our students, encourage collaboration, and showcase the talent and creativity that exists within our school. I look forward to seeing our young mathematicians engage with enthusiasm and make us proud with their efforts.

Let this competition be a celebration of curiosity, perseverance, and the joy of learning.

Sincerely,

Competition Categories & Format

Junior Division PYP(3-5)

  • Buzzer Round (x3): Fun mental math and puzzles. +10 for correct, -5 for wrong.
  • Rapid Fire Round: 2 minutes per team. +10 per correct, no negatives.

Intermediate Division MYP(6-8)

  • Written Test: 20‚Äì25 IQ & logical reasoning questions. Top scorers form finalist teams.
  • Final Rapid Fire: 2 minutes per team. +10 per correct, no negatives.

Advanced Division (O/A Levels, DP & High School)

  • Research Challenge: Teams solve an open-ended math problem and present findings.
  • Presentation & Opposition: Debate-style critique with Reporter, Opponent, Reviewer, Observer roles.
  • Final Rapid Fire: 120 seconds per team. +10 per correct, no negatives.

Advance Division Structure

Team Formation: A team consists of 3 to 4 students.

Problem Release: 3 challenging math problems of varying difficulty are provided to the teams two-three weeks before the competition:

  • Level 1: For O1, O2, 9th, and 10th
  • Level 2: For O3, 10th, 11th
  • Level 3: For A1, A2, 12th, DP

Solution Preparation: Teams must prepare detailed solutions to these problems in advance of the competitions.

Research Debates: The core of the tournament involves structured research debates where teams take turns in specific roles.

Roles in Debate Rounds

  • Reporter: Presents the main ideas and results obtained from solving the problem.
  • Opponent: Analyzes the Reporter's solution, pointing out inaccuracies and errors in the paper and presentation.
  • Reviewer: Evaluates the performance of both the Reporter and the Opponent.
  • Observer: Makes important remarks or observations that may have been missed by the other roles.

Objectives

Stimulate Interest in Mathematics: To foster interest in mathematics and its applications.

Develop Skills: To cultivate scientific thinking, communication skills, and teamwork among students.

Promote Exchange: To support social and cultural exchange between high school students in a stimulating environment.

Scoring System

  • Junior: Buzzer 50%, Rapid Fire 50%
  • Intermediate: Written Test 60%, Rapid Fire 40%
  • Advanced: Research 50%, Presentation/Opposition 30%, Rapid Fire 20%

Code of Conduct

To ensure a fair, respectful, and productive environment for all participants and staff, the following code of conduct must be observed:

Respect: Treat all participants, organizers, judges, and staff with courtesy and respect.

Integrity: Uphold the highest standards of academic honesty. Cheating, plagiarism, or any form of academic dishonesty will not be tolerated and may result in disqualification.

Sportsmanship: Engage in fair play and demonstrate good sportsmanship at all times. Celebrate success and handle challenges with grace.

Adherence to Rules: Follow all competition rules, instructions, and schedules provided by the organizers.

Appropriate Conduct: Maintain a respectful and conducive environment for learning and competition. Avoid disruptive behavior.

Use of Resources: Use only permitted resources during competition rounds. Unauthorized materials or devices are strictly forbidden.
